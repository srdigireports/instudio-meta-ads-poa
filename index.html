02 — The Opportunity

Why Meta Ads
Make Sense Now

  • 01 Mosman is a high-income, high-intent suburb. Median household income in the Mosman LGA is among Sydney's highest. Women aged 25–54 in this catchment spend significantly above the national average on personal care. A targeted Meta campaign puts In Studio directly in front of this audience where they already spend time — Instagram especially.
  • 02 The beauty vertical converts well on Meta. Industry-wide, beauty brands achieve a CPA of approximately A$40–50 for a new appointment booking at Australian CPM rates. For a studio with an average package value of A$90–130, that's a profitable first visit before accounting for any repeat bookings or lifetime value.
  • 03 Training courses are a high-margin Meta opportunity. Course-level lead generation on Meta typically costs A$15–35 per enquiry in the beauty education niche. With a conversion rate of 15–20% from enquiry to enrolment and an estimated course value of A$400–700, the projected ROAS on training ads alone is 4x–6x.
  • 04 Instagram Reels and Stories are purpose-built for this content. Before/after brow and lash transformations are among the most shared beauty content formats on Instagram. Since In Studio's services are cosmetic (not medical), there are no AHPRA advertising restrictions — meaning before/after creative, client results, and testimonials are all fully available for use in ads.
  • 05 Ingrid's personal brand is a built-in creative asset. A founder-led studio with a Colombian heritage story, an artist's background, and a clear aesthetic sensibility is ideal for the kind of authentic, trust-building content that outperforms polished corporate creative on Meta.

04 — Campaign Architecture

3-Phase Campaign
Structure

Phase 01
Foundation & Testing
Weeks 1 – 4

Install the pixel, structure the account, launch 2–3 creative variants across two ad sets, and let Meta's algorithm gather learning data. This phase is intentionally conservative. The goal is clean data, not maximum bookings.

Install Meta Pixel on instudiolash.com + Gettimely confirmation page
Set up Conversions API (CAPI) via Gettimely or server-side if available
Define custom conversion: "Book Now" click on Gettimely as a Purchase/Lead event
Launch Campaign 1 — Services (Traffic/Bookings objective)
Launch Campaign 2 — Training (Lead Gen objective, WhatsApp CTA)
Test 2 creative angles: Personal brand (Ingrid) vs. Result (before/after)
Use Advantage+ Audience targeting (let Meta optimise from day 1)
Weekly creative performance review — pause underperformers at 7 days
Monthly Spend Target A$1,200 / month
Phase 02
Optimise & Retarget
Weeks 5 – 8

By week 5 the pixel has 4+ weeks of data. Scale the winning creative from Phase 1, introduce a retargeting layer, and begin building Lookalike Audiences. Shift the Services campaign from Traffic to a Bookings/Conversions objective.

Analyse Phase 1 CTR, CPC, and click-to-booking rate — identify top creative
Scale winning ad set budget +20–30%, pause losers
Launch retargeting campaign — website visitors + IG engagers (30 days)
Build 1% Lookalike off IG engagers (Services); 1% LAL of Training leads
Introduce Packages as a specific ad unit (Ultimate Makeover / Deluxe)
Switch Services campaign objective to Booking Conversions if pixel has 50+ events
Test Reels placement (expected 26% lower CPC vs. Feed)
Add seasonal hook if applicable (Spring/Summer lash prep, Mother's Day etc.)
Monthly Spend Target A$1,500 / month
Phase 03
Scale & Diversify
Weeks 9 – 12

With two months of conversion data, a tested creative library, and warm audiences in place, this phase scales the highest-ROAS campaigns and introduces a dedicated Training acquisition funnel with a lead magnet (e.g., a free guide PDF or intro Reel).

Scale top-performing Services ad set by 20% weekly (never more than 25% at once)
Launch dedicated Training funnel: Awareness → Lead (WhatsApp/DM)
A/B test training lead magnet: "How to start a lash lifting business" guide
Deploy 2–3% Lookalike audiences (broader reach at competitive CPM)
Test Advantage+ Shopping Campaigns for package promotions (30–40% CPA reduction)
Build seasonal content calendar for Q4 (Christmas gift bookings, end-of-year events)
Produce monthly reporting dashboard (bookings attributed, cost per booking, ROAS)
Review budget ceiling — recommend increasing to A$2,500+/month if ROAS holds above 3x

07 — Tracking & Measurement Setup

Getting the
Foundation Right

Clean tracking is the most important first-week task and the foundation everything else is built on. Without a functioning pixel and conversion event, Meta cannot optimise for bookings — it will optimise for clicks to anyone, which wastes budget fast.

01
Install Meta Pixel on instudiolash.com

Pixel goes in the site header via WordPress/Elementor. Fires on every page visit. This is the base layer — required before anything else.

02
Set Up a Custom "Book Now Click" Conversion Event

Since Gettimely is a third-party booking system (bookings.gettimely.com), we cannot fire a Purchase event on confirmation. Instead we fire a Lead event on the "Book Now" button click. This gives Meta a clear signal to optimise towards. Once Gettimely or a booking confirmation email can pass data back, we upgrade this to a full Purchase event.

03
UTM Parameters on All Ad Links

Every ad URL must include UTM tags (utm_source=meta, utm_medium=paid, utm_campaign=services-phase1) so booking source can be tracked in Google Analytics (if active) and reported to Ingrid. Minimum: source, medium, campaign, content.

04
Conversions API (CAPI) — Phase 2

As iOS privacy restrictions reduce browser pixel reliability, CAPI sends server-side events directly from the website to Meta. Increases event match quality and reduces data loss. Set up via Meta's native CAPI gateway or through a partner integration. Target: event match quality score of 7+ out of 10.

05
Custom Audience Pixel Triggers

Set up a custom audience for "Website Visitors — Last 30 Days" and "Website Visitors — Last 7 Days" as soon as the pixel is live. These audiences are needed for retargeting in Phase 2 and need 30 days of data to be usable.

06
Monthly Reporting — Bookings Attributed

We will track: ad spend, impressions, clicks, cost per click, booking events fired, estimated bookings, and revenue attributed. Delivered as a monthly summary alongside a simple ROAS calculation. Ingrid will need to manually cross-check Gettimely booking volume against pre-/post-launch baselines to validate incremental lift.
